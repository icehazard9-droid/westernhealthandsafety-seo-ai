# Indoor Air Quality Assessments Calgary

## Service Overview

Western Health & Safety provides CIH-led Indoor Air Quality Assessments for commercial, industrial, institutional, healthcare, educational, municipal, and multi-unit residential buildings throughout Calgary, Alberta, and British Columbia.

Unlike standalone air sampling, an Indoor Air Quality Assessment is a systematic investigation of the indoor environment. The assessment evaluates building systems, ventilation performance, moisture conditions, occupant concerns, and potential contaminant sources before determining whether environmental sampling is appropriate.

Our objective is to identify the root causes of indoor environmental concerns and provide practical, evidence-based recommendations that improve occupant health, comfort, and building performance.

---

## Assessment Process

A typical Indoor Air Quality Assessment may include:

- Initial consultation
- Building history review
- Occupant interviews
- Visual inspection
- HVAC evaluation
- Ventilation assessment
- Moisture investigation
- Temperature and humidity measurements
- Carbon dioxide and carbon monoxide measurements
- Particulate and VOC measurements where appropriate
- Targeted air or surface sampling
- Accredited laboratory analysis
- Interpretation of findings
- Written report with recommendations

---

## When an Assessment is Recommended

Indoor Air Quality Assessments may be appropriate when buildings experience:

- Persistent occupant complaints
- Unexplained odours
- Water damage
- Suspected mould
- Poor ventilation
- Indoor comfort concerns
- Renovation-related complaints
- Chemical odours
- Sick building concerns
- Real estate due diligence

## Indoor Air Quality Assessment vs Testing

Indoor Air Quality Testing measures specific contaminants or environmental conditions.

An Indoor Air Quality Assessment is a comprehensive investigation that determines why indoor environmental concerns exist and whether testing is required.

Testing is one component of a professional assessment rather than a replacement for it.
