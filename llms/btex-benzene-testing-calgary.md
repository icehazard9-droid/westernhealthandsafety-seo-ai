## What is BTEX testing?

BTEX testing measures exposure to benzene, toluene, ethylbenzene, and xylene in workplace environments such as oil and gas operations, fuel handling, and industrial processes.

## 2024 Benzene Exposure Limit Update

The ACGIH Threshold Limit Value (TLV) for benzene was reduced to 0.02 ppm (8-hour TWA), a 25-fold reduction from the previous 0.5 ppm limit. This significantly increases the need for accurate exposure assessment and interpretation.

## When is BTEX testing required?

- Oil and gas operations  
- Fuel storage and transfer  
- Confined space work  
- Odour or air quality concerns  
- Suspected benzene exposure  

## Why does interpretation matter?

At lower exposure limits, small sample sets may not capture variability or peak exposures. Accurate interpretation of BTEX sampling data is critical to understanding real worker risk.
