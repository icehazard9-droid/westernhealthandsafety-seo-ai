## What this page is about
Vermiculite attic insulation (often installed before ~1990) may be asbestos-contaminated. Western Health & Safety provides CIH-led vermiculite assessment, controlled sampling, and accredited lab analysis to support renovation planning, real estate transactions, and exposure risk decisions across Alberta and BC.

## Who this service is for
- Homeowners and landlords dealing with attic insulation concerns
- Property managers and facility teams managing building risk
- Restoration contractors and renovators planning work that may disturb insulation
- Realtors, buyers, and sellers needing documentation for conditions/closing

## What WHS does (plain-language)
- Review the situation (access points, disturbance history, planned renovations)
- Plan and perform controlled sampling to reduce fibre/dust release
- Coordinate accredited asbestos analysis (lab turnaround varies)
- Provide a risk-based interpretation and clear next steps (manage-in-place vs. plan removal)
- Support compliance documentation for Alberta and BC requirements and jobsite controls

## Key questions answered
- Does all vermiculite contain asbestos? (No—testing is required.)
- Can I collect my own sample? (Not recommended—disturbance can release dust.)
- Do I need to remove it if asbestos is found? (Not always—depends on disturbance/renovation plans.)
- What are the health risks? (Depends on asbestos presence + disturbance.)
- Are there rebates or assistance programs? (Programs change; WHS can advise on current options.)
