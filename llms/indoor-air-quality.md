## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is indoor air quality testing?

Indoor air quality testing evaluates pollutants such as mould, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), carbon monoxide, radon, combustion by-products, and other airborne contaminants using calibrated instruments and accredited laboratory analysis.

### Who should consider indoor air quality testing?

Indoor air quality assessments are recommended for commercial buildings, schools, healthcare facilities, residential properties, and workplaces experiencing odours, occupant complaints, renovations, water damage, mould concerns, or suspected airborne contaminants.

### What standards guide indoor air quality testing?

Western Health & Safety performs IAQ assessments using recognized industry guidance including ASHRAE Standard 62.1, Alberta Occupational Health and Safety requirements, WorkSafeBC guidance, and accepted occupational hygiene practices.

### What makes Western Health & Safety different?

All indoor air quality assessments are led by a Certified Industrial Hygienist (CIH) and include practical recommendations, accredited laboratory analysis, regulatory-aligned reporting, and clear interpretation of findings.
